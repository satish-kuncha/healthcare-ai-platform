SECTION 1: APPOINTMENT SCHEDULING, CANCELLATION, AND LATE ARRIVALS

1.1 Standard Scheduling Windows
All routine consultations with General Practitioners must be booked at least 48 hours in advance via the patient portal or telephone triage line. Specialist appointments, including those for Cardiology, Orthopedics, and Neurology, operate on a rolling 14-day booking window. No emergency walk-ins are permitted for specialized departments; emergency cases must be rerouted immediately to the trauma center located at the East Wing facility.

1.2 High-Demand Time Slots
Time slots spanning Monday afternoon between 2:00 PM and 4:00 PM, as well as Friday mornings from 8:00 AM to 10:30 AM, are designated as High-Demand Slots. These intervals are strictly reserved for critical follow-up examinations and multi-disciplinary case reviews. Front-desk personnel are prohibited from assigning these hours to standard diagnostic queries or initial patient intakes without explicit written sign-off from the Administrative Director.

1.3 Cancellation Penalty Framework
Patients must notify the administration office of any cancellation or rescheduling request at least 24 hours prior to their slotted time. Failure to provide a 24-hour notice will result in an automated late cancellation fee of $75 billed directly to the patient's account. This fee is strictly an administrative penalty and cannot be billed to, or reimbursed by, any private or corporate health insurance policy. Exceptions are granted only for documented medical emergencies or acute transit disruptions verified by the operations manager.

1.4 Late Arrival and Forfeiture Protocol
If a patient arrives more than 15 minutes past their scheduled appointment time, the session is officially classified as a "Late Forfeiture." The medical practitioner is under no obligation to see the patient past this window, as doing so compromises the scheduling integrity of subsequent sessions. A late forfeiture incurs the standard $75 late fee, and the patient must re-queue via the standard portal. If the practitioner chooses to accommodate a late arrival, the consultation will terminate precisely at the originally scheduled time, and no extension will be permitted.


SECTION 2: INSURANCE COMPLIANCE, BILLING, AND REIMBURSEMENT MATRIX

2.1 Tiered Insurance Architecture
The clinic recognizes three primary categories of private health insurance policies, each carrying distinct authorization rules and co-pay liabilities:
- Tier A (Premium Corporate - Codes starting with 'INS-1'): Covers 100% of all diagnostic imaging, routine consultations, and advanced sub-specialist interventions. All co-pay liabilities are fully waived under this tier. Orthopedic consultations, cardiac stress tests, and high-resolution MRI scans are pre-authorized.
- Tier B (Standard Individual - Codes starting with 'INS-4'): Covers basic medical care and standard laboratory diagnostics at 100%. However, specialized consultations, physical therapy, and out-of-hospital imaging require a rigid 20% out-of-pocket co-payment from the patient at the time of service.
- Tier C (Basic Auxiliary - Codes starting with 'INS-7'): Covers emergency stabilization and general physician check-ups only. All specialty departments are considered out-of-network, requiring upfront payment.

2.2 Out-of-Network and Non-Traditional Treatment Exclusions
Homeopathic treatments, acupuncture, holistic wellness counseling, and alternative chiropractic therapies are completely excluded from coverage across all insurance tiers, including Tier A. Patients electing these services must settle their balances fully upfront at the billing desk according to the baseline fee schedule. The clinic will not submit itemized claims to insurers for alternative medicine.

2.3 Diagnostic Imaging Pre-Authorization Flow
Advanced imaging procedures, specifically Computed Tomography (CT) scans, Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI), and Positron Emission Tomography (PET) scans, require an insurance pre-authorization token code prior to execution. For Tier B and Tier C policies, obtaining this token takes between 3 to 5 business days. The billing department will initiate the authorization claim immediately upon receiving a signed practitioner referral, but patients are advised not to schedule their imaging slots until the token code is successfully generated and logged in the system.


SECTION 3: CLINICAL OPERATIONS, MEDICINE DISPENSING, AND PHARMACOPOEIA

3.1 Prescription Drug Dispensing Rules
The clinic pharmacy dispenses medications strictly in accordance with the standardized Indian Pharmacopoeia (IP) frameworks and statutory drug schedules. Schedule H and Schedule X medications, including specialized psychotropic agents, neurological stimulants, and high-potency analgesics, require a physically signed, dual-stamped prescription from a registered clinic specialist. Digital signatures or verbal verifications are fundamentally insufficient for dispensing these specific controlled substances.

3.2 Generic Substitution Policy
Unless a prescribing physician explicitly marks a prescription with the directive "Dispense Brand As Written" (DBAW), the internal pharmacy will automatically substitute brand-name pharmaceuticals with their bioequivalent generic alternatives listed in the national formulary. This measure ensures cost optimization for the patient and maintains compliance with corporate care guidelines. Generic substitutions possess identical active pharmaceutical ingredients (APIs), dosage strengths, and bioavailability profiles as their branded counterparts.

3.3 Multi-Medication Coordination and Allergy Screening
Prior to generating any new pharmaceutical order, the clinic's Electronic Health Record (EHR) system automatically performs a cross-reference allergy sweep against the patient's centralized profile database. If an active ingredient or structural compound triggers a known allergy flag (e.g., a patient with a documented Penicillin or Sulfa Drug allergy being prescribed an alternative antibiotic compound), the order is immediately locked. Overriding a database allergy lock requires a secondary administrative passcode and a written justification note logged by the chief attending physician.
